# Project names

## Proposed names

### Craft and mastery

`tekton` — Greek *tektōn* (builder, craftsman). Root of *architect*; mastery of construction at every level of abstraction.

`tecton` — Variant of *tekton*, sharper sound. Same register of structural craft.

`daedalon` — Greek *Daidalos* (legendary master craftsman). Mythological archetype of inventive, multi-domain craftsmanship.

`daedala` — Derived from *Daedalus*. Craft, invention, and design intelligence.

`daidala` — From Greek *daidalos* (cunningly, intricately wrought) and *daidala* (skillfully crafted works). Elaborate, multi-component construction.

`hephaestia` — Greek *Hēphaistos* (divine smith and artificer). The forge as disciplined creation; imposing form on raw material.

`harmon` — Evokes *harmonia* (fitting together). Coherent design and harmonious organization.

`domus` — Latin (house, dwelling). Architecture as inhabitable structure.

`fundra` — Coined, evokes *fundus* (foundation). Structural grounding.

`arcus` — Latin (arch). Architectural elegance and stability.

`forma` — Latin (form, shape). Shaping principles and design.

`nodus` — Latin (knot, connection). Structural relation and binding.

`poiema` — Greek *poiēma* (a thing made; a crafted artifact). From *poiein* (to make), the root of *poetry* and *poiesis*. The compiled document as a deliberate, structured artifact of a generative process.

### Structure and form

`axion` — Evokes *axis* (central line). Principle and line of organization.

`dromos` — Greek *dromos* (a defined course, passageway, processional avenue). Directed flow of a compilation pipeline; also an architectural term (the processional corridor of a Greek tomb or temple).

`nexum` — Latin *nexum* (a binding obligation, a legal bond of connection). Dependency linking and inter-stage coupling.

`plegma` — Greek *plégma* (anything woven or plaited; a network structure). Graph-like, interlocking
topology of a multi-stage pipeline.

`schema` — Greek *skhēma* (form, structure, arrangement). Design patterns, architecture, and conceptual structuring.

`skopos` — Greek (aim, target, purpose). Intentional design and methodological direction.

`strata` — Latin (layers). Organization across levels.

`temenos` — Greek (bounded, sacred enclosure). Organized, delimited space; literary register.

### Order, rule, and standard

`axioma` — Greek (foundational principle, axiom). Self-evident starting points.

`critera` — Evokes *criterion* (standard of judgment). Evaluation and quality standards.

`ethos` — Greek (character, habitual disposition). Professional culture and orientation.

`kanon` — Greek *kanōn* (straight rod, rule, standard). Authoritative norms and best practices; orthographically distinct from *canon*.

`kanonix` — Modernized form of *kanon*. Same register, more contemporary feel.

`norma` — Latin (carpenter's square, rule, pattern). The standard against which practice is evaluated.

`gnomon` — Greek *gnōmōn* (indicator, knower; sundial pointer). Dual valence: the knower and the indicator.

`taxis` — Greek *taxis* (arrangement, ordered disposition, marshalling of elements). Determinate ordering of interdependent compilation steps; pipeline orchestration.

`nomos` — Greek (law, convention). Normative order.

`recta` — Latin (correctness, straightness). The proper way.

`regula` — Latin (rule, guideline). Normative prescription.

`metron` — Greek (measure, standard, proportion). Quality criteria, design discipline, and balance.

`ordinis` — Latin, from *ordo* (order, arrangement). Architectural discipline and process quality.

`ordo` — Latin (order, arrangement, system). Direct form of *ordinis*.

`modus` — Latin (method, mode, procedure). Operational manner.

### Foundation and principle

`organon` — Greek *organon* (instrument, tool). Aristotle's term for his logical corpus; a systematic instrument of correct reasoning.

`vitruvion` — From *Vitruvius*, author of *De Architectura*. Classical systematization of architectural principles (*firmitas, utilitas, venustas*).

`principia` — Latin (first principles, foundations). The axiomatic layer beneath all practice.

`pragma` — Greek *pragma* (deed, act, thing accomplished). Root of *pragmatic*; actionable, validated practice.

`praxis` — Greek (practice, applied method). Knowledge proven through doing.

### Knowledge and pedagogy

`stoa` — Greek (covered colonnade; place of teaching and discourse). Tutorials, principles, and methodological exposition.

`archeion` — Greek (archive, place of records). A reference corpus of practices and standards.

`didaxa` — Coined from *didaktikos* (apt at teaching). Explicitly pedagogical register.

`gnoma` — Greek *gnōmē* (maxim, judgment). Condensed principles and wisdom.

`noema` — Greek *noēma* (thought, meaning). Conceptual content and reflective understanding.

`sophon` — Greek *sophos* (wise). Disciplined knowledge and wisdom.

`glossa` — Greek *glōssa* (tongue, commentary). Explanation and annotation.

`ennoia` — Greek *ennoia* (concept, notion). Reflective understanding.

`logia` — Greek *-logia* (discourse, organized knowledge). A body of systematic knowledge.

`paideia` — Greek *paideia* (formation, education, cultivation). Classical ideal of intellectual formation.

`schole` — Greek *skholē* (leisure devoted to learning). Source of *school*; study and reflection.

`prisma` — Greek *prisma* (something sawn, prism). Multiple facets of understanding and presentation.

### Coined

`synthra` — Constructed name in the style of *morpha* or *tessara*, suggesting synthesis and structure.

`plektex` — Coined from Greek *plekō* (to plait, braid, weave) + *-tex*. Braiding etymology maps to dependency graph traversal and recipe interleaving; the TeX suffix signals the domain.

`ergotex` — Coined from Greek *ergon* (work, task, artifact) + *-tex*. Build work performed on/for TeX; strongest functional transparency of the TeX-composite candidates.

### Documentation and commentary

`scholion` — Greek *scholion* (scholarly note, marginal annotation, explanatory comment). Directly aligned with documentation as commentary and structured explanation; suitable for a documentation-focused linter.

`krinon` — From Greek *krinein* (to separate, judge, decide). Emphasizes evaluation, discrimination, and rule-based judgment; appropriate for a linter that assesses documentation quality.

`dokimon` — From Greek *dokimē* / *dokimos* (test, proof, approved after examination). Conveys validation, testing, and approval against standards.

`hermeneia` — Greek *hermēneia* (interpretation, exposition, explanation). Suggests attention to meaning and clarity in written exposition; suitable for a documentation analysis tool.

`stigma` — Greek *stigma* (mark, punctuating sign, distinguishing mark). Evokes the act of marking issues in text; semantically relevant to linting, though modern connotations may be less desirable.

`orthos` — Greek *orthos* (straight, correct, upright). Expresses correctness, rectitude, and conformity to proper form; well suited to a style or documentation checker.

`diorthon` — From the Greek semantic field of correction and emendation, related to *diorthōsis* (setting straight, correcting, revising). Suggests textual correction and formal improvement.
